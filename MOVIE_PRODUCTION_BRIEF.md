# PUHC Innovation Alleys - Movie Production Brief
**Duration:** 2-3 Minutes  
**Project:** Water Alley Digital Twin Platform  
**Location:** Pico-Union, Los Angeles (Between W 11th & W 12th St)

---

## PROJECT OVERVIEW (15-20 seconds)

### The Problem
Los Angeles has over 900 miles of underutilized alleys that are:
- Heat-exposed with no shade
- Lacking community infrastructure
- Disconnected from neighborhood activity
- Safety concerns and neglect

### The Solution
Water Alley serves as a **prototype transformation** demonstrating how alleys can become:
- Community gathering spaces
- Climate-resilient corridors
- Cultural expression zones
- Food production sites

---

## WATER ALLEY - THE PROTOTYPE (20-25 seconds)

### Location Details
- **Address:** Between W 11th & W 12th Street, Pico-Union
- **Coordinates:** 34.04982619528969, -118.2818586747943
- **Theme:** Water (stormwater management, rain gardens, bioswales)
- **Length:** ~300 feet
- **Width:** Varies 12-20 feet

### Four Infrastructure Zones
1. **Area A** - North Entrance (residential gateway, white fencing)
2. **Area B** - Mid-North / Red Trellis Zone (widest section, 18-20 feet)
3. **Area C** - Mid-South / Mural Zone (concrete walls, existing murals)
4. **Area D** - South Exit / Rear Zone (garage structures, large walls)

---

## THREE DESIGN INTERVENTIONS (45-60 seconds)

### 1. SHADE STRUCTURES (Infrastructure)
**Applied to:** Areas B and C

**Components:**
- Overhead solar canopy system (energy generation)
- Vertical trellises (climbing plants, privacy)
- Decorative water medallions (cultural identity)

**Impact:**
- 15°F temperature reduction
- 5kW solar capacity
- 85% shade coverage
- 20-year lifespan

**Key Visual Assets:**
- `photo-3.webp` - Overhead canopy frame looking north
- `solar-canopy-concept-new.png` - Shade sail design concept
- Before/after comparison renders

---

### 2. COMMUNITY MURALS (Cultural)
**Applied to:** Areas C and D

**Themes:**
- Water-themed marine life (jellyfish, octopus, sea turtles)
- Cultural expression and community ownership
- Building on preserved existing murals

**Impact:**
- 200+ linear feet of wall art
- Community-designed themes
- Cultural identity reinforcement

**Key Visual Assets:**
- `photo-5.webp` - Water-themed murals on alley walls
- `photo-4.webp` - Iron fencing with murals
- `mural-1.webp`, `mural-2.webp`, `mural-3.webp` - Detail shots

---

### 3. URBAN FARMING (Food Security)
**Applied to:** Areas A and B

**Components:**
- Raised beds with vertical trellis support
- Tiered pyramid planters for herbs
- Modular trellis systems
- Stacked vertical garden towers

**Impact:**
- Fresh produce for 20+ families
- Community building and education
- Heat reduction through vegetation

**Key Visual Assets:**
- `photo-2.webp` - Container gardens with planter boxes
- `urban-farming-object-1/2/3/4.webp` - Individual components
- `urban-farming-objects-with-plants.png` - Complete system with mature plants

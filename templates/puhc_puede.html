Research-Driven Urban Design

PUHC PUEDE Alleys

Research, Mapping, and Design for Environmental Justice in Pico-Union

PUHC PUEDE combines research, GIS mapping, and design intervention to reimagine Pico-Union alleys through environmental justice, climate resilience, and community health. Grounded in the AURA report and supported by ArcGIS spatial data, this project translates research into site-specific alley design strategies for South Los Angeles.
1

Research Foundation

The AURA report provides the research framework for understanding environmental justice, climate resilience, and community health in the project area. This external supporting report establishes the analytical foundation for design intervention.

Source: AURA Engine
PUHC PUEDE Alleys: Architecture and Urban Design for Environmental Justice, Climate Resilience, and Community Health in South Los Angeles Census Tracts 2098.10 and 2095.20

This report originates from AURA Engine and is included as a supporting research document for the PUHC PUEDE project. It provides critical analysis of environmental conditions, community health indicators, and climate vulnerabilities specific to the Pico-Union census tracts.

Key Research Themes

Environmental Justice

Analysis of environmental inequities and community exposure to urban heat, pollution, and inadequate green infrastructure in Pico-Union.

Climate Resilience

Strategies for adapting alley corridors to address climate impacts including heat islands, stormwater management, and extreme weather events.

Community Health

Documentation of health implications related to environmental conditions and the potential for design interventions to improve public health outcomes.

Tract-Specific Analysis

Detailed examination of Census Tracts 2098.10 and 2095.20, providing localized data and context for targeted design responses.

Design Relevance

Research-backed recommendations for alley transformation that address documented environmental and social conditions.

3

Design Response

The Alley 3 prototype represents the actionable design solution informed by both the AURA report and mapped ArcGIS conditions. This site-specific intervention addresses documented community and environmental conditions through three integrated strategies.

Shade Structures

Integrated system including overhead solar canopies, vertical trellises, and decorative water medallions. Responds to low tree canopy coverage and urban heat island effects documented in GIS data and AURA research.

View Details

Community Murals

Cultural expression and community ownership through public art. Addresses environmental justice and community health by creating spaces for cultural identity and social cohesion in underserved areas.

View Details

Urban Farming

Container gardens for food security and community building. Responds to climate resilience needs and community health priorities identified in the AURA report, providing local food access and green infrastructure.

View Details

Each intervention is calibrated to address specific environmental deficits revealed by GIS analysis and community needs documented in the AURA research. The design translates data and research into tangible spatial improvements that enhance climate resilience, environmental justice, and community health.
